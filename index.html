Common questions

Parents Ask Us
What is the minimum age for Class 1 in CBSE schools?+
As per CBSE norms, a child must be at least 6 years old as of 31st March of the admission year to enrol in Class 1. For Pre-KG the minimum is 3 years, LKG is 4 years, and UKG is 5 years. Private affiliated schools may set higher minimum ages but cannot go below the CBSE minimum.
How does the KV lottery system work?+
When more children apply than seats available within a priority category, KV conducts a computerised lottery. The lottery is transparent and results are published on the KVS portal. Priority 1 (service children) seats are filled first, then Priority 2, and so on. Lottery is held category-wise, not across all applicants together.
What is the difference between CBSE and ICSE boards?+
CBSE (Central Board of Secondary Education) is run by the central government, follows NCERT syllabus, and is accepted across all central universities. ICSE (Indian Certificate of Secondary Education) is run by CISCE, has a broader syllabus with more emphasis on English. CBSE is better for competitive exams like JEE/NEET; ICSE is better for students going abroad or pursuing humanities.
Which scholarships are available for CBSE Class 8 students?+
The most important scholarship for Class 8 students is NMMS (National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship), which provides ₹12,000/year for Class 9–12. Students from economically weaker sections with at least 55% marks in Class 7 are eligible. The state-level exam is held every year in November–December.
Can a civilian child get admission in Army Public Schools?+
Yes, civilian children can get admission in Army Public Schools (APS) but only in the general category, which is limited to 25–30% of total seats after defence children are accommodated. RMS (Rashtriya Military Schools) also reserves a small percentage of seats for civilian boys. The entrance exam for APS is the All India Army Public Schools Common Entrance Exam (AISCE).
What happens if a child fails the CBSE Class 10 board exam?+
If a student fails in 1–2 subjects in CBSE Class 10, they are eligible for the Compartment Exam held in July. If they pass the compartment exam, they receive a passing certificate. If they fail in 3 or more subjects, they must repeat Class 10. CBSE also has an improvement exam option for students who want to improve their marks.
